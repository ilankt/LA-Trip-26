A WEEK ON THE COAST

Los Angeles
& Santa Barbara

May 19 – 26, 2026

Seven days. Two cities. One road between them.

↓

The Shape of the Week

A week in Los Angeles doesn't have to mean seven days in Los Angeles. The flight from Tel Aviv lands us in a city that's as much a collection of neighborhoods as it is a single place — and the coast just north of it, up the Pacific Coast Highway, opens into something quieter: Santa Barbara, a town with Spanish-tile roofs and a working waterfront, and behind it the Santa Ynez valley, thirty minutes inland, where the vineyards start.

So the plan is this. Four days in Los Angeles — the Getty, the Academy Museum, Griffith at sunset, and a morning in Beverly Hills before the Sunset Strip. Then on Saturday we pick up a car and drive north on the Pacific Coast Highway: lunch at Malibu Pier, a stop at El Matador Beach for the sea stacks, and two nights at Hotel Californian on State Street. One of those days is for Santa Barbara itself — the Mission, the waterfront, and not much else.

“The drive up the coast
is itself the good part.”

Sunday afternoon we drive back. Two more days in LA — downtown this time, The Broad and Grand Central Market — and then Venice on the way to the airport. No wasted days. No zig-zagging. The Santa Barbara leg is the good surprise in the middle.

Santa Monica Pier, dusk

DAY 1 — TUE MAY 19

Landing slowly

We land mid-afternoon, already tired, and we don't pretend otherwise. Check in, drop the bags, walk the Santa Monica boardwalk until the sun is gone. Dinner somewhere we can walk to. The rest of the week can wait.

Must Do

  • Walk to the end of the pier before sunset — the Pacific goes orange from out there
  • Keep walking south along the beach bike path as the sky changes colour
  • Dinner on Main Street or Ocean Ave — both are a 10-minute walk from the pier
  • 1 LAX arrival — LY 5 2:55 PM
  • 2 Hotel check-in, Santa Monica ~5:00 PM
  • 3 Santa Monica Pier sunset walk Evening

Venice Beach — morning

Robert Irwin's Central Garden, The Getty

DAY 2 — WED MAY 20

Venice in the morning, the Getty in the afternoon

Start early in Venice — Gjusta for breakfast, then a slow walk down Abbot Kinney. You're based in Santa Monica so this is practically next door. Uber across to the Getty Center by midday: Richard Meier's travertine campus on a hill above the 405, tram access, the best garden in the city. Half a day here is the right amount. Dinner back in Brentwood or Santa Monica.

Must Do

  • Gjusta at 8:30 AM (320 Sunset Ave, Venice) — go before 9 AM or after 10:30 to avoid the queue. Order at the counter, find a spot outside
  • Abbot Kinney only — don't walk toward the beach boardwalk; the scene there is rough and not worth it. The boulevard itself is calm and good
  • At the Getty: take the tram up, do the Central Garden, then the west terrace for the Pacific view. Don't miss the garden — it's the best thing there
  • 1 Gjusta, Venice320 Sunset Ave, Venice — before 9 AM or after 10:30 AM 8:30 AM
  • 2 Abbot Kinney BoulevardAbbot Kinney Blvd, Venice — stay on the boulevard, skip the boardwalk 10:00 AM
  • 3 The Getty Center1200 Getty Center Dr, Brentwood — tram from Level 2 parking 1:30 PM
  • 4 Dinner in Brentwood or Santa Monica Evening

DAY 3 — THU MAY 21

Film history, then the city from above

Optional first stop: a quick pass through Hollywood Blvd — 30 minutes, see the TCL Chinese Theatre and a stretch of the Walk of Fame, then leave. It's touristy and can feel rough on the side streets; treat it as a checkbox, not a destination. Then the real day: the Academy Museum is the best museum in LA right now, and Griffith Observatory timed for 6:30 PM catches both the last of the light and the full city-lights transition.

Must Do

  • Hollywood Walk of Fame (optional) — 30 minutes max, stay on Hollywood Blvd between Highland and Vine, don't wander side streets, then Uber out. It's worth seeing once; it's not worth lingering
  • The Academy Museum Sphere — the glass globe on the Wilshire building is the most striking piece of architecture built in LA in years
  • Griffith at 6:30 PM: sunset is around 7:55 PM in late May, so you arrive with golden light, watch the city turn purple, then see the full grid of lights appear below
  • 1 Hollywood Walk of Fame & TCL Chinese Theatre (optional)Hollywood Blvd at Highland — 30 min, stay on the main boulevard, Uber in and out 10:00 AM
  • 2 Lunch on Larchmont BoulevardLarchmont Village — Le Pain Quotidien, Republique nearby, or any patio 1:00 PM
  • 3 Academy Museum of Motion Pictures6067 Wilshire Blvd, Mid-Wilshire — book timed ticket in advance 2:30 PM
  • 4 Griffith Observatory2800 E Observatory Rd, Los Feliz — Uber up, parking fills fast; arrive 6:30 PM for golden hour into city lights 6:30 PM

DAY 4 — FRI MAY 22

Old money, then the Strip

Beverly Hills in the morning is one thing — Rodeo Drive is worth a walk, but the real neighbourhood is the residential streets behind it, lined with jacarandas and cars that cost more than apartments. Lunch on Beverly Drive. Then west into West Hollywood, where Sunset Boulevard becomes the Strip: the Chateau Marmont, the billboard canyon, rooftop bars with views across the city. Car-free day throughout — Uber everywhere.

Must Do

  • Walk Canon Drive and Bedford Drive off Rodeo — the actual Beverly Hills feeling lives on these residential blocks, not on the tourist strip itself
  • The Chateau Marmont exterior at least — the building you recognise from every film set in LA, still looks exactly like it should
  • Book Soup on Sunset Strip — best bookshop in LA, open until 11 PM, one of the Strip's genuinely good institutions
  • 1 Rodeo Drive & Beverly HillsRodeo Dr, Beverly Hills — walk the golden triangle: Rodeo, Wilshire, Canon 10:30 AM
  • 2 Lunch on Beverly DriveN Beverly Dr, Beverly Hills — Il Pastaio, Nate 'n Al's, or any patio you find 1:00 PM
  • 3 Sunset Strip, West Hollywood8000–9000 Sunset Blvd — Chateau Marmont, Book Soup, Employees Only for a drink 3:30 PM
  • 4 Dinner in West HollywoodGracias Madre on Melrose, or Craig's — both walkable from the Strip 7:00 PM

Pacific Coast Highway, California

El Matador State Beach, Malibu

DAY 5 — SAT MAY 23

The drive is the day

Pick up the rental car this morning — the first time we actually need one. Mid-morning, head north on PCH. The Pacific Coast Highway from Santa Monica to Santa Barbara is two and a half hours of cliffs and water, and there are two stops worth making. Lunch on the pier at Malibu Farm. An hour at El Matador Beach, where the sand gives way to sea stacks and caves. Check in at Hotel Californian by evening — one block from the beach, on the edge of the Funk Zone — and walk up State Street for dinner.

Must Do

  • El Matador: walk north along the sand to the sea arch and sea cave — only reachable at low tide, worth checking the tide chart in advance
  • Malibu Farm: sit at the outdoor tables at the end of the pier, not inside — the ocean is literally underneath you
  • Arrive Santa Barbara by 6 PM — the Funk Zone (Yamazaki, Municipal, El Yauco) is walkable from the hotel and open until 8
  • 1 Pick up rental car & depart Santa MonicaPick up from Santa Monica or WeHo — avoid LAX surcharges 10:00 AM
  • 2 Malibu Farm Pier Cafe23000 Pacific Coast Hwy, Malibu — sit outdoors at the end of the pier 12:30 PM
  • 3 El Matador State Beach32350 Pacific Coast Hwy, Malibu — sea stacks, cave, rocky coves at low tide 2:30 PM
  • 4 Hotel Californian check-in36 State St, Santa Barbara — Funk Zone, 1 block from the beach 5:00 PM

DAY 6 — SUN MAY 24

The Queen of the Missions, then south

One easy morning in Santa Barbara before the drive back. The Old Mission is the city's founding building — Spanish colonial, ochre stucco, a long garden, and a view down to the ocean from the forecourt. Brunch at Helena Avenue Bakery. On the road by two, back in LA by four, dinner at Bestia or Bavel in the Arts District — whichever has the earlier reservation.

Must Do

  • Arrive the Mission at 10 AM before tour groups — the rose garden and the cemetery behind the church are quietest then
  • Walk around to the forecourt: the view south from there, over the palm trees down to the harbour, is one of the best in Santa Barbara
  • Helena Avenue Bakery: pastry plate, coffee, eat outside on the small front patio — 131 Anacapa St, Funk Zone
  • 1 Old Mission Santa Barbara2201 Laguna St, Santa Barbara — Spanish colonial, twin towers, rose garden & cemetery 10:00 AM
  • 2 Brunch at Helena Avenue Bakery 11:30 AM
  • 3 Drive back to LA — 101 South via Ventura & Camarillo~2 hrs · Santa Barbara to Arts District · no toll roads 2:00 PM
  • 4 Dinner: Bestia or Bavel, Arts DistrictBestia: 2121 E 7th Pl · Bavel: 500 Mateo St — both walkable from each other ~7:00 PM

DAY 7 — MON MAY 25

Contemporary, concrete, and market lunch

The Broad is an oddity — a contemporary art museum inside a perforated honeycomb shell across from Disney Hall. Free timed ticket, one hour inside. Grand Central Market is a two-minute walk: a hundred years old and still the best lunch in downtown. Then Uber to the Arts District — do not walk between these two areas. The streets in between run through a rough part of downtown; the Uber is three minutes and a few dollars.

Must Do

  • Pre-book The Broad online — tickets are free but timed and fill weeks ahead; walk-up is hit-or-miss
  • Grand Central Market: Eggslut window or Wexler's Deli for lunch — eat at the counter, not at a table
  • Uber from Grand Central Market to the Arts District — do not walk; the route on 6th Street goes through Skid Row. Three-minute ride, no discussion
  • 1 The Broad221 S Grand Ave, Downtown — free timed ticket, book online 2 weeks ahead 11:00 AM
  • 2 Grand Central Market317 S Broadway, Downtown — 2-min walk from The Broad, open since 1917 1:30 PM
  • 3 Arts District — galleries, murals, coffeeUber from GCM — do not walk. Mateo St, Traction Ave, E 3rd St are the good blocks 3:00 PM
  • 4 Dinner: Bestia or BavelBestia: 2121 E 7th Pl • Bavel: 500 Mateo St — both in the Arts District 7:00 PM

Arts District, Los Angeles — morning

DAY 8 — TUE MAY 26

One last morning

A slow breakfast in the Arts District — Bread Lounge for pastries, or Urth Caffé if you want eggs. A short walk to see the murals on the blocks around Traction Ave and E 3rd Street. Then straight to LAX. The 110 South from downtown is the cleaner route and avoids the worst of the 405. Flight home at 1:30 PM.

Must Do

  • Bread Lounge (700 S Santa Fe Ave) — the best pastries in the Arts District, no queue in the morning
  • Walk the mural blocks: Traction Ave, E 3rd St, and Hewitt St have the best street art in LA — all within a few minutes of each other
  • Leave by 10:30 AM — take the 110 South to the 105 West for LAX; cleaner than the 405 on a Tuesday morning
  • 1 Breakfast — Bread Lounge or Urth Caffé700 S Santa Fe Ave, Arts District — no stress, no queue 8:30 AM
  • 2 Arts District mural walkTraction Ave, E 3rd St, Hewitt St — 20 minutes on foot 9:30 AM
  • 3 Uber to LAXTake 110 South → 105 West — ~30 min without traffic; leave by 10:30 AM 10:30 AM

Two Nights · May 23 & 24

Hotel Californian — Spanish-Moorish architecture, foot of State Street

Harbor view from the rooftop

Spanish Colonial detail, State Street

Old Mission, five minutes north

Funk Zone tasting rooms, walkable

Hotel Californian sits at the foot of State Street, on the edge of the Funk Zone and one block from the beach. The architecture is Spanish-Moorish — arched doorways, tiled floors, wrought-iron balconies — and the rooftop pool looks over the harbor. Walkable to two dozen restaurants, tasting rooms, and coffee shops. Rated 4.3 stars across 550+ reviews. The hotel runs a shuttle further up State Street when we want to venture out.

  • Rooftop pool with harbor views
  • Funk Zone location — walkable everywhere
  • Spa on-site
  • Complimentary shuttle up State Street
If this one is booked: Belmond El Encanto — hillside, more secluded, classic Santa Barbara. A quieter choice if we want to splurge.
